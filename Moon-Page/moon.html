THE EMPTY FIELD

At 0 light-seconds you have only entered an empty field.


Everything in life is energy, including the infrared radiation and biophotons you give off. The light emitted by our bodies is low and likely to be scattered and absorbed by the Earth's atmosphere. But even then it is amazing to think that the light emitted from your body at the moment of birth is still traveling through space, and it will continue to do so for an unimaginable amount of time.

I invite you to follow this hypothetical timeline of your archaeological photons.

TheㅤSunㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤ

At 493 light-seconds ${you will have/you} reached the Sun.


It exists at the heart of our solar system, a fiery ball of gas that illuminates our world and provides energy that sustains life on Earth. Reaching millions of degrees through nuclear fusion, it will continue to thrive for 5 billion years.

The Sun is everybody’s first birth star, and will remain so until they turn 4.

JUPITERㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤ

At 52 light-minutes ${you will have/you} reached Jupiter.


Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, with over 70 moons. Its huge gravitational force protects us by intercepting many asteroids that could overwise hit Earth. It’s Great Red Spot is a massive storm three times the size of Earth that's been raging for 300 years.

VOYAGER 1ㅤㅤㅤ

At 22 light-hours ${you will have/you} reached Voyager 1.


This spacecraft has traveled farther than any other man-made object. Launched in 1977, it finally entered interstellar space in 2012. Onboard is the Voyager Golden Record, a “message in a bottle” to any space-faring civilizations that might encounter it in the future.

OORT CLOUD

At 2 light-years ${you will have/you} reached the Oort Cloud.


Located at the end of our solar system, this region is a vast cloud of icy objects that hold the remnants from the original creation of our solar system. The comets we see today were birthed here.

ALPHA CENTAURI

At 4.3 light-years ${you will have/you} reached Alpha Centauri.


This triple star system, home of Alpha Centauri A, Alpha Centauri B, and Proxima Centauri, has captured the interests of astronomers for years now. At only 4.37 light-years, it is the closest star system to our home.

ROSS 248ㅤㅤㅤㅤ

At 10 light-years ${you will have/you} reached Ross 248.


This dim red dwarf star is one of the closest stars to us, it is a flare star and is known for dramatic magnetic activity. It is of great interest because it potentially hosts habitable planets.

EQ PEGASIㅤㅤㅤㅤ

At 20 light-years ${you will have/you} reached EQ Pegasi.


EQ Pegasi is a binary star system with two small red dwarfs. They are known for their unpredictable brightness; due to a giant field of dust, their luminosity fluctuates frequently.

GAMMA PAVONIS

At 30 light-years ${you will have/you} reached Gamma Pavonis.


Also known as Pavo, Gamma Pavonis is a main-sequence star, similar in size and mass to our Sun. At 1.3 billion years old, Pavo is a relatively young star and is used in the study of stellar evolution.

MU ARAEㅤㅤㅤㅤ

At 50 light-years ${you will have/you} reached Mu Arae.


Mur Arae is a multi-planetary system, and is home to four known planets. It was here that the first super-Earth was discovered, one with a mass 14 times larger than Earth’s.

NU DRACONIS

At 100 light-years ${you will have/you} reached Nu Draconis.


Nu Draconis, also known as Kuma, is a white main-sequence star. In 2002, a planet was discovered orbiting around the star, called Nu Draconis b, it was a gas giant with a mass several times that of Jupiter.

K2-155dㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤ

At 200 light-time ${you will have/you} reached K2-155d.


This tidally locked exoplanet orbits a red dwarf star with an orbital period of 40 days. It lies within the habitable zone of its star meaning that it has the favorable conditions for liquid water to exist on its surface. It is currently a contender for extraterrestrial life.

PLEIADES CLUSTER

At 444 light-years ${you will have/you} reached the Pleiades star cluster.


Also known as the Seven Sisters, this cluster of stars is very famous and easily recognizable in the night sky. Actually consisting of hundreds of young stars born within 100 million years, it has had a prolific presence in mythology and folklore over the millennia.

ORION NEBULAㅤㅤㅤㅤ

At 1,344 light-years ${you will have/you} reached the Orion Nebula.


The Orion nebula is one of the brightest and most recognizable nebulae in the night sky, being visible just below Orion’s belt with the naked eye. This giant cloud of gas and dust is a stellar nursery which gives us lots of insight into the birth of stars.

MILKY WAYㅤㅤㅤㅤ

At 27,700 light-years ${you will have/you} reached the MilkyWay Galaxy.


The milky way is home to our solar system, this magnificent galaxy is 13.6 billion years old and contains hundreds of billions of stars, planets, and other celestial objects.

The mass of the black hole in its center is equivalent to 4 million Suns. Its intense gravitational forces bend and distort light.

MAGELLANIC CLOUD

At 158,200 light-years ${you will have/you} reached the Large Magellanic Cloud.


This cloud is a satellite galaxy of the milky way. This irregular dwarf galaxy is home to the giant Tarantula nebula and has been the testing bed for many important scientific discoveries.

ANDROMEDA

At 3 million light-years ${you will have/you} reached the Andromeda Galaxy.


This grand-spiral galaxy is our nearest massive galaxy. Its relationship to our Milkway is fascinating because these two galaxies are moving towards each other at 110 km/s and are expected to collide and merge in 4.5 billion years, forming a new, larger galaxy that some have dubbed as “Milkomeda”.

MESSIER 87ㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤ

At 53 million light-years ${you will have/you} reached Messier 87.


In 2019 the EHT released the first ever image of a black hole at the center of this supergiant elliptical galaxy Messier 87. The image was produced by a global research team using observations from a worldwide network of radio telescopes. The new view captures light bent by the powerful gravity of the black hole, which is four million times more massive than our Sun.

GREAT ATTRACTOR

At 147 million light-years ${you will have/you} reached The Great Attractor.


The Great Attractor is the most mysterious gravitational anomaly in the observable universe. It is believed to be a massive concentration of dark matter and galaxies pulling many nearby galaxies including our own. The study of this attractor is very important because of its insight into the large-scale structure of the universe as well as the nature of dark matter.

SLOAN GREAT WALL

At 1 billion light-years ${you will have/you} reached the Sloan Great Wall.


This massive cosmic structure is a complex and intricate network of galaxies, gas, and dark matter that stretches more than a billion light-years across. It is home to thousands of very old and distant galaxies.

GREAT GRB RING

At 9 billion light-years ${you will have/you} reached the Great GRB Ring.


The Great Gamma Ray Burst Ring is named after the intense gamma-ray bursts that are some of the most powerful explosions ever observed in the universe. These explosions are caused by a rare kind of galaxy collision that triggered a wave of thousands of short-lived stars and their subsequent collapses along with their black hole remnants.
